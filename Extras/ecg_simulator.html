EXextras · synthetic biosignals

Simulate ECG Data.

This simulator synthesises a single-lead electrocardiogram from a PQRST Gaussian-sum model, with additive noise sources representative of surface acquisition: low-frequency baseline wander attributable to respiration and electrode motion, broadband electromyographic interference, narrowband powerline coupling with its odd harmonics, transient electrode artefacts, and Gaussian thermal noise.

The resulting waveforms can be exported as CSV or TXT for use in MATLAB, Excel, or NI Multisim, supporting end-to-end simulation of your device. In Multisim, the TXT file is typically loaded into a piecewise-linear voltage source, which then drives the analog front-end during transient analysis.

The clean reference is constructed as a superposition of Gaussians parameterised to reproduce the P, Q, R, S, and T deflections, repeated at the prescribed heart rate with physiologically plausible beat-to-beat interval variability. Each noise component is generated independently and additively superimposed on the clean waveform.

CSV — columns time_s, ecg_mV, ecg_clean_mV, noise_mV, one sample per row, for use in analysis environments such as MATLAB or Python.

TXT — two-column time (s) voltage (V) ASCII file in the format accepted by NI Multisim's PIECEWISE_LINEAR_VOLTAGE source. Instantiate a PWL voltage source in Multisim and set its data-file parameter to the downloaded file to drive the analog front-end with the synthesised waveform during transient analysis.
