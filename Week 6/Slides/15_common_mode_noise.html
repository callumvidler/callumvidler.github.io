Common-mode signal
Voltage that appears identically on both inputs (e.g. 50 Hz mains hum). Because it's the same on V⁺ and V⁻, subtraction cancels it.
Differential signal
The voltage difference between the two inputs is the wanted signal (e.g. ∼1 mV ECG). An instrumentation amp amplifies only this.
Common-Mode Rejection Ratio
The ratio of differential gain to common-mode gain. Measures how well the amp rejects common-mode noise relative to the wanted differential signal. Higher CMRR = cleaner output.
Ad = differential gain
Acm = common-mode gain
