Shows the Ag/AgCl electrode–electrolyte interface. Cl⁻ ions in the body participate in the half-cell reaction at the AgCl surface. Electrons flow through the metal wire while ions carry charge in the electrolyte.

E = +0.222 V
Ag/AgCl half-cell potential (vs SHE)
Equilibrium — no net current

Half-Cell Reaction

AgCl + e⁻ ⇌ Ag + Cl⁻

At the Interface

Reduction (← ): AgCl gains an electron, releasing Cl⁻ into the electrolyte and depositing Ag.

Oxidation (→ ): Cl⁻ from electrolyte combines with Ag, forming AgCl and releasing an electron into the wire.

E° = +0.222 V vs Standard Hydrogen Electrode. This stable potential makes Ag/AgCl ideal for biopotential recording.
